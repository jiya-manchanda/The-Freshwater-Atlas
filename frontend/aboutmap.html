Chapter 1: A World of Water

Water is the foundation of life, shaping landscapes and sustaining ecosystems. The Freshwater Atlas begins by highlighting the vital role of Orange County's freshwater resources. With over 500 named lakes, countless rivers, and natural springs, this region is a hub for aquatic biodiversity and an essential source of water for both wildlife and humans.

Each water body has a unique story. For example, Lake Apopka, once known as a pristine fishing destination, has been the focus of major restoration efforts to combat agricultural runoff. Rivers like the Wekiva flow through lush forests, offering habitats for manatees and other species while providing recreational opportunities for locals and tourists alike.

By understanding these water systems, we can better appreciate their significance and the need to preserve them for future generations.
